About

Hello — I'm glad you're here.

Sai Krishna

Writer · Human · Imperfect

I am a writer based in Hyderabad, India. I have spent the last few years trying to understand India through reading and reporting.

My work lives at the intersection of the personal and the empirical. I write personal essays about memory, culture, and what it means to pay attention. And I write data stories that try to put numbers in conversation with lived experience.

I started this blog because I was tired of writing that disappears: tweets that scroll away, articles that live behind paywalls, newsletters that pile up unread. I wanted a place that is mine, unhurried, and built for the kind of reader who doesn't mind slowing down.

"The world is not made of atoms. It is made of stories."
— Muriel Rukeyser (roughly)

I am particularly drawn to questions about how ordinary life in India, the kind that doesn't make headlines, is changing. How people move. What they read. Why certain cities grow and others hollow out. What happens to languages when their speakers leave for somewhere else, and so on.

Background

Before this blog, I studied Masters in Public Policy at National Law school of India University, Bengaluru, and I now write full-time at Factly Media and Research, parent organisation for dataful.in, which is a terrifying and occasionally wonderful way to live.

On the Blog

Essays — Personal, long-form pieces on culture, memory, books, cinema, language, and attention. Mostly about what it means to be alive.

Data Stories — Reported pieces using publicly available data, from NSSO, Census, IMD, MOSPI, and other sources, to look at India's transformations through numbers, maps, and charts.

Get in Touch

I love hearing from readers. The best way to reach me is by email at weforchange009@gmail.com. I read everything; I reply when I can.
